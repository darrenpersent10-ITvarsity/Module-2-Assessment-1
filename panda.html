PANDA

The Giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) also known as Giant Pandas is the native to south central China. It is easily recognized by the large distinctive ears, and across it's round body. The name "giant panda" is sometimes used negatively to the panda. Though it belongs to the order Carnivora, the giant panda's diet it good they will occasionally eat other grass, wild tubers, or even meat in the forest and they may receive honey, eggs, fish, yams, shurb leaves, oranges, or banana's.

The Giant panda lives in a few mountain ranges in China and also in neighbouring provinces, namely Shaanxi and Gansu. As a result of extinctionthe giant panda has been driven out of lowland areas where it once lived.

The Giant panda is a conservation reliant endangered species.
