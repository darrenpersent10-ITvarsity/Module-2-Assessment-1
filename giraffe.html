GIRAFFES

The giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) is an African even-toed terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. Its species name refers to its camel-like colouring. Its chief distinguishing characteristics are its extremely long neck and long tongue its distinctive coat patterns. It is classified under the family Giraffidae, along with okapi. The nine subspecies are distinguished by their coat patterns.

The giraffe's scattered range extends from Chad in the north from Niger in the west to Somalia in the east. Giraffes usually inhabit savannahs, grand primarly food is acacia leaves, which they browse at heights most other herbivors can't reach.

Giraffes are preyed on by lions; their calves are also targeted by African wild dogs. Adult giraffes do not have strong social bonds, though they do gather happen to be moving in the same directions and establish social hierarchies combat bouts where the neck is used as a weapon responsibility for raising the young.
